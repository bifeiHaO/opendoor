INSTRUCTIONS

In Agora 1.0, you will get 4 cards which is respectively 1 Customer, 2 Plan with This and 1 Role Play.

  • With these 4 cards, there are totally three things you need to do.
  • First, it’s better for you to brainstorm ideas in groups or individually according to the card which indicates customer.
  • Then, you need to think about potential problems for customers and plan solutions for them with the cards -- Plan with This and Role Play.
  • Finally, you’re supposed to present your idea through answering questions.

Following are some important hints and tips for you to better enjoy Agora 1.0.

  • To begin with, the guideline leading the formulation of business plans is to have enough room for business growth. In this case, your business can be seen like a start-up and is possible for your business to be fantastic, realistic, and scalable and quickly gains traction.
  • In the process of problem positioning,
  • Valuable problems should be popular, growing and urgent.
  • Popular means this problem is faced by many people. Growing will give this business more and more customers. Urgent means the positioning of problems is sharp and accurate.
  • Also, you need to ask yourself Is it a mandatory and expensive problem? And Recurring and frequent problems?
  • In the process of thinking about solutions,
  • your solutions must be directly related to motivation (your reason to identify the problem), Ability (Your business idea) and Trigger (Your solution should trigger the consumer to incorporate your solution into their lives).
  • Besides, you need some raw data to support the feasibility of the solution, which may come from focus groups, product-market-fit test or something.
  • After brainstorming and early-stage idea generation, you can reflect your idea by following these questions.
  • 1. What are your insights from the initial raw data?
  • 2. What is your competitive advantage over other competitors in this space? Does your competitive advantage have these (great to have multiple but must have at least two).
  • 3. Is your founder extremely specialized in the respective field your business is entering?
  • 4. Is the market you are entering growing at a rapid pace e.g. 20% YoY growth?
  • 5. Product advantage (is your product 8-10x times better?)
  • 6. Do you have a monopoly? Or can you have a monopoly?
